Welcome ABESEC

Domestication: Cats were domesticated about 9,000 years ago, likely in the Near East, where they helped humans by hunting rodents.

Communication: Cats communicate not just by meowing, but also through body language—like tail position, ear movement, and purring. Interestingly, adult cats usually "meow" only to humans, not to other cats.

Sleepyheads: Cats spend 12 16 hours a day sleeping! They re crepuscular, meaning most active during dawn and dusk.

Hunting instinct: Even well-fed cats love to "hunt" toys, bugs, or random moving objects because of their strong predatory instincts.

Our Old Pets

Rapid Growth: Puppies grow really fast—most double their birth weight in the first week. By 6–8 months, many already look like mini versions of their adult selves. Baby Teeth: Just like humans, puppies have baby teeth (28 of them) that later fall out, replaced by 42 adult teeth.

Teeth that never stop growing: A rabbit’s teeth grow continuously throughout its life (about 2–3 mm per week!). That’s why they need hay, wood chews, or veggies to wear them down. Super hearing: Their long ears aren’t just cute—they can rotate almost 270° to pick up sounds from far away, helping them detect predators.
